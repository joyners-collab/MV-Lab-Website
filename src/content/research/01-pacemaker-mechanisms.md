---
title: "Mechanisms of cardiac pacemaker function"
order: 1
summary: "How do specialized cells in the sinoatrial node generate the rhythmic activity that drives every heartbeat? We dissect the ion channels, calcium handling, and gene-regulatory programs that produce robust, adaptable pacemaking."
---

The sinoatrial node is the body's most robust oscillator, generating an
electrical impulse every beat for a lifetime. We use a combination of
electrophysiology, imaging, and molecular biology to understand how
pacemaker cells achieve this remarkable stability while remaining capable
of rapid, dynamic modulation.
