---
title: "Comparative cardiac biology across vertebrates"
order: 2
summary: "From hummingbirds beating 1,200 times per minute to hibernating ground squirrels at 5 bpm, vertebrate hearts span an extraordinary dynamic range. We exploit this natural diversity to uncover conserved and divergent mechanisms of heart rate control."
---

Across vertebrates, heart rate varies by more than three orders of magnitude
— yet the core architecture of the pacemaker is remarkably conserved. By
studying species at the extremes, we identify the molecular adaptations
that enable specialized cardiac performance.
